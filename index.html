Health and Demography in the United States

Heain Yee - March 2020
Explore relationships between health risks and particular demographics.
Correlations Discovered Between Health Risks and Poverty, Age, Income

Poverty

  • States with higher poverty rates are more likely to have higher obesity rates.
  • States with higher poverty rates are more likely to have higher percentage of population who smoke.
  • States with higher poverty rates are more likely to have percentage of population who lack healthcare.

Age

  • States with higher median age are more likely to have higher obesity rates.
  • States with higher median age are more likely to have higher percentage of population who smoke.
  • States with higher median age are less likely to have percentage of population who lack healthcare.

Income

  • States with higher median household income are less likely to have higher obesity rates.
  • States with higher median household income are less likely to have higher percentage of population who smoke.
  • States with higher median household income are less likely to have percentage of population who lack healthcare.
